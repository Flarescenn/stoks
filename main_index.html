Beginner

Equity

Equity = Assets - Liabilities

The ownership interest in a company's assets after deducting its liabilities. It represents the residual interest in the assets of a company, also known as shareholders' equity.

P/E Ratio

Evaluates the valuation of a company's stock by comparing its current market price per share to its earnings per share (EPS).

Assets

Assets = Liabilities + Shareholders' Equity

Resources owned or controlled by the company that have economic value and are expected to provide future benefits.

Outstanding shares

Outstanding shares refer to the total number of shares of a company's stock that have been issued and are held by investors, including both common and preferred shares. These shares are actively traded on the stock exchange

Dividend

Dividend refers to a reward, cash or otherwise, that a company gives to its shareholders.

Repo Rate

Assets = Liabilities + Shareholders' Equity

"Repo rate," short for "repurchase agreement rate," refers to the interest rate at which central banks lend money to commercial banks or other financial institutions for a short-term period.

Intermediate

ROE

ROE=net income X 100/shareholders equity

It indicates how effectively a company is utilizing its equity to generate profits.
ROE measures the return generated by a company's equity shareholders on their investment.

Market cap

Market cap= current share price X Total Outstanding shares

Market capitalization (market cap) is a measure of the total value of a publicly traded company's outstanding shares of stock. It is calculated by multiplying the current market price of a single share by the total number of outstanding shares.

Book Value

Book value= total assets- total liabilities

Book value, also known as shareholders' equity or net asset value, is a financial metric that represents the total value of a company's assets minus its liabilities. It indicates the net worth of the company attributable to its shareholders.

Face value

Equity = Assets - Liabilities

Face value, also known as par value, is the nominal value or stated value assigned to a financial instrument, such as a bond, preferred stock, or sometimes common stock. It represents the value of the instrument as stated on the document itself and is typically set by the issuer.

TTQ

Represents the total number of shares or contracts of a security that have been traded during a specified period, such as a trading day. Traders and investors may use total traded quantity to assess liquidity, volatility, and trading opportunities in the market.

Liquidity

Liquidity refers to the ease with which an asset or security can be bought or sold in the market without significantly affecting its price. It is a measure of how quickly and efficiently an asset can be converted into cash without causing a significant change in its value. In that sense cash itself is the most liquid.
